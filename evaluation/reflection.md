# Reflection

## 1. What did AI do well in the Requirements Engineering process?

AI was useful for creating structured drafts, organizing information into tables, generating stakeholder questions, and transforming elicitation findings into requirements and user stories. It also helped maintain a consistent format across multiple documents.

## 2. What mistakes or assumptions did AI often make?

AI sometimes used vague words such as "fast", "easy", and "secure" without measurable criteria. It also tended to add assumptions that were not directly supported by the case. Some initial outputs were too general and needed stronger traceability to stakeholder sources.

## 3. How did changes to SKILL.md improve output quality?

Adding clearer rules, quality checks, source ID requirements, and failure conditions improved the AI output. The AI became more consistent when the skill explicitly required requirement IDs, measurable criteria, assumption labels, and traceability links.

## 4. Why is human review still necessary?

Human review is necessary because AI can produce plausible but unsupported statements. Requirements Engineering affects real project decisions, so the final baseline must be checked for correctness, feasibility, clarity, consistency, and business value by a human reviewer.

## 5. How does traceability help maintain consistency?

Traceability helps connect stakeholder needs, elicitation sources, requirements, user stories, acceptance criteria, and priorities. It makes it easier to identify the impact of changes and prevents requirements from becoming disconnected from stakeholder needs.

## Final Reflection

AI is helpful as an assistant for drafting and organizing Requirements Engineering artifacts, but it should not replace human judgment. The student remains responsible for validating assumptions, resolving conflicts, and ensuring the final requirements baseline is accurate and traceable.
